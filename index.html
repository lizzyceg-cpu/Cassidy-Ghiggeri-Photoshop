How to Double expose an image in photoshop on an Ipad

Time to learn: 30 minutes
Understanding rating: 30% understanding

Ever struggle to double expose an image in photoshop on an Ipad but find its different then on a computer? It's really easy to do when you know how. Why don't you try to it yourself? Follow the steps and you'll understand how to double expose an image!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Ipad
  • Photoshop account
  • Patience
  • Apple Pencil (reccomended)
  • 2 photos
  • Time

  1. First sign in or make an account for photoshop.

    You will need to get a subscription if you don't already have one.

  2. Pick you base picture that you want for this project.

    For this you can typically pick which ever picture you want.

  3. Add another layer.

    And import your next picture.

  4. Scale up your second picture so it fully covers the first.

    This is necessary to make sure your double exposure lays properly.

  5. Hide the 2nd layer.

    And lasso your main object on the first layer.

    6. While the first layer is still masked click on the second layer.

    So you can see the outline of your object throught the second layer.

  6. Once you do that press the layer mask button.

    Now bring the opacity back up to 100%.

    7. Now change the mode to lighten and then click back on the firs† layer.

    Now we have to equalize both photos to do that we must make them black and white to do that you will click on add clip and select the black and white option.

  7. Bring the opacity of the first photo down slightly and adjust the exposure.

    So you can see the image underneath.

    8. Now you just play around with the exposure of both images.

    Until you find what you like then you are all done.
